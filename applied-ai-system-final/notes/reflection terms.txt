Constraints are the rules or limits the system must respect. Think of it like packing a suitcase — you can only fit so much. In PawPal+, the constraint is time: the owner only has 90 minutes, so not every task can make the cut.

Priorities are how you rank what matters most when you can't do everything. If the suitcase is full and you have to choose between packing shoes vs. a snack, priority tells you which one wins. In PawPal+, feeding is high priority (the pet must eat), playtime is low priority (nice to have).

Tradeoff means every choice has a cost. When you pick one thing, you give something else up. In PawPal+, the tradeoff is: by always doing high-priority tasks first, you guarantee the important stuff gets done — but you might run out of time before getting to the fun, lower-priority tasks. You traded completeness for correctness.

One-sentence version of each:

Constraint = a limit you cannot break
Priority = what you do first when you can't do it all
Tradeoff = what you give up in exchange for what you gain
